In the next steps you will get Introduction how to bring an existing roboter-setup to life.

Step1: Power on the Robot

First you may Troubleshooting: Check the Wires. To power on the robot afterwards, you had to press the green button (see The Hardware Setup). The switches 1 (EPOS2) and 2 (Laserscanner) right of the power button should be switched on.

Step2: Waiting until the robot is ready

Some LEDs are switch on others are off. If the robot get not into this state within 5 minutes lock at Troubleshooting: The Meaning of the LEDs.

Step3: Drive the robot

There are different driving-Modes by using the gamepad. When the robot doesn't move, you can switch between this modes by pressing the "RB"-Button of the Logitech F710. For other gamepads it's the .... button.

driving-mode: One Stick for each side

With the left stick you set the velocity for the left side of the robot and with the right stick for the right side of the robot.

driving-mode: Velocity and Turning with two sticks

The right Stick (only up and down) set the forward velocity and the left stick (only left and right) set the angular velocity of the robot.

driving-mode: Velocity and Turning with one stick

Same as driving-mode: Velocity and Turning with two sticks but both directions (up - down & left - right) are set with one stick.

Last Step: Power off the robot

After driving you can power off the robot by press and hold the red power button until the Lights of the Power-panel are switched off.

Troubleshooting: Check the Wires

The Controll-PC (see The Hardware Setup) should be connected to one of the EPOS2s and to the Laser-Scanner. Both are connected by USB. The "Nano Receiver" of the Controller (Logitech F710) should also Plugged into one USB-Port.

Troubleshooting: The Meaning of the LEDs

Here some known LED-settings, the corresponding errors and their solution.

LED: red is on

Power off the robot (Last Step: Power off the robot), Troubleshooting: Check the Wires, and switch on again (Step1: Power on the Robot).
